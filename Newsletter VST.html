The Challenges Of Digital

 Transformation

Digital transformation in the financial services sector is just as tricky as it is evolutionary. For most financial institutions, digitization offers a chance to acquire new customers, and offer better products and services. It also holds the key to becoming more efficient in an innovative and disruptive environment. The coronavirus pandemic led to a surge in the adoption of digital services within the banking sector. While this has proved challenging for the digital players, opportunities abound. For instance, as opposed to only digitizing existing business processes, accelerating areas such as open banking will boost digital transformation within the industry. According to Independent Banking & Payment expert Johannes Busse, banking will be in a state of continuous digital transformation, adapting to the changing technological landscape. “In retail banking, it is about creating a smooth digital customer journey from KYC to banking functionalities to instant loan payouts,” he said. Whether it’s adapting to new demands in the market, dealing with legacy systems or improved technological solutions, the financial landscape is impacted in several ways. Hence, it must face this head on to achieve a successful digital transformation.

Here are some of the biggest challenges of digital transformation and how to tackle them.
   
 

Investment and Funding

Aleksander Khomich, CEO of Andersen, told Forbes that while a bank is implementing the active phase of transformation, the level of investment that creates tangible results is about 10% of the bank's annual expenditure. This means that without constant financial support, the process may be fragmented. The reality for developing economies is such that the smaller mid-tier banks and credit unions have limited tech budgets and resources. Without access to both core platforms and fintechs, they will miss out on all the innovation that a digital-native bank can easily adopt. It’s also difficult for a lot of IT directors to justify and obtain investments. One simple way out of this would be to focus on highlighting the benefits before cost. This could include productivity growth and how much the bank will save.

 





















Competition

In terms of customer experience, the competition is no longer between banks or with fintechs alone but also the entire tech space at large. With world players such as Google and Amazon using digital transformation to make big strides, specialization in the industry is fast growing. For more financial institutions to stay ahead of this, they need to be better equipped for integrations with third parties. It may also be wise to partner with other vendors to increase automation. Also, financial institutions can increase trust by providing scalable and uninterrupted services that will encourage a shift to digital currencies.

Competency and gap in customer expectations

The challenge, from an IT perspective, involves meeting the expectations of customers. It is no longer about protecting funds alone as user experience has changed in recent times. Financial institutions can prepare for upcoming transformation by identifying new services for customers and unique ways to leverage cloud services and establishing partnerships to boost seamless integration. Training staff and investing in new tools is also crucial to automate and simplify its processes; digitalization can only be successful if it reaches every aspect of the entity.



Data Management and cloud computing

Active data management helps to attract new customers and build trust. It can also be used to design personalized experiences for each customer. The challenge lies in ensuring that, regardless of the channel they use, they can manage their financial operations. Cloud computing is similar, but it is mostly targeted towards information storage features. The technology also allows for flexibility and the advantage of smaller costs. However, there is still a lot to consider and implement before cloud computing systems are 100% secure.



Cyber Security

This is one of the most critical challenges faced by digital transformation in the banking sector. The objectives of cyber security range from detecting threats to preventing fraud against clients. The financial institutions that prioritize cyber security are more likely to adapt to the changes driving digital transformation. According to experts, investments in cyber security should be divided between prevention, detecting threats, and alleviating the effects of external interference.

Regulations

Digital transformation comes with the challenge of new and updated regulations. But there’s an advantage because it becomes easier for financial institutions to comply using GDPR, for example. This usually works better when they update their data management practices..

 
Overall, there isn’t one single solution to the challenges linked to digital transformation. Limited budgets, support from the top, and regulatory requirements are some of the problems financial institutions face. But for this digital journey to be worthwhile, staff and other stakeholders must be well equipped and ready to drive change within the industry.
